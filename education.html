The Many Wonderful Phases of the Moon

Phases of the Moon

Phase Duration Description
New Moon 1-3 days The first phase of the moon is the new moon. This phase is when the moon is not visible from Earth. The new moon occurs when the moon is between the Earth and the sun. The new moon is the first phase of the moon, and it is also the beginning of the lunar cycle. The new moon is the best time to start new projects, set new goals, and make new plans. It is a time of new beginnings and fresh starts.
Waxing Crescent 3-7 days The second phase of the moon is the waxing crescent. This phase is when the moon is partially visible from Earth. The waxing crescent occurs when the moon is between the new moon and the first quarter moon. The waxing crescent is the second phase of the moon, and it is a time of growth and expansion. It is a time to take action and move forward with your goals and plans.
First Quarter 7-10 days The third phase of the moon is the first quarter. This phase is when the moon is half visible from Earth. The first quarter occurs when the moon is between the waxing crescent and the waxing gibbous. The first quarter is the third phase of the moon, and it is a time of action and progress. It is a time to take the next step in your goals and plans and move forward with confidence.
Waxing Gibbous 10-14 days The fourth phase of the moon is the waxing gibbous. This phase is when the moon is more than half visible from Earth. The waxing gibbous occurs when the moon is between the first quarter and the full moon. The waxing gibbous is the fourth phase of the moon, and it is a time of growth and development. It is a time to build on your progress and continue moving forward with your goals and plans.
Full Moon 14-17 days The fifth phase of the moon is the full moon. This phase is when the moon is fully visible from Earth. The full moon occurs when the moon is on the opposite side of the Earth from the sun. The full moon is the fifth phase of the moon, and it is a time of completion and fulfillment. It is a time to celebrate your achievements and accomplishments and to acknowledge your growth and progress.
Waning Gibbous 17-21 days The sixth phase of the moon is the waning gibbous. This phase is when the moon is more than half visible from Earth. The waning gibbous occurs when the moon is between the full moon and the last quarter. The waning gibbous is the sixth phase of the moon, and it is a time of reflection and introspection. It is a time to review your progress and make any necessary adjustments to your goals and plans.
Third Quarter 21-24 days The seventh phase of the moon is the third quarter. This phase is when the moon is half visible from Earth. The third quarter occurs when the moon is between the waning gibbous and the waning crescent. The third quarter is the seventh phase of the moon, and it is a time of release and letting go. It is a time to release any negative energy or emotions and to let go of anything that is holding you back.
Waning Crescent 24-28 days The eighth phase of the moon is the waning crescent. This phase is when the moon is partially visible from Earth. The waning crescent occurs when the moon is between the third quarter and the new moon. The waning crescent is the eighth phase of the moon, and it is a time of rest and renewal. It is a time to recharge your energy and prepare for the next lunar cycle.

What is a Lunar Eclipse?

During a lunar eclipse, Earth comes between the Sun and the Moon, blocking the sunlight falling on the Moon. There are two kinds of lunar eclipses:

  • Partial Lunar Eclipse
  • Total Lunar Eclipse

During some stages of a lunar eclipse, the Moon can appear reddish. This is because the only remaining sunlight reaching the Moon at that point is from around the edges of the Earth, as seen from the Moon's surface. From there, an observer during an eclipse would see all Earth's sunrises and sunsets at once.
